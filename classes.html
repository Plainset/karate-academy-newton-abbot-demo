Classes & Timetable

Classes for every age, 7 days a week

Members can train at any class that suits them — there are no contracts, and you can train as often as you wish.

Age groups

Little Warriors, Juniors, and Adults

Little Warriors (4–7)

Pre-martial-arts classes with their own grading system, built around co-ordination, balance, and learning to follow instructions. Shorter sessions to suit young attention spans, taught by Sensei Janet and Sensei John.

Juniors (from age 5)

Children learn karate from age 5 onwards, with free trial lessons so families can see the classes are the right fit. A “Juniors Only” environment builds discipline, confidence, and self-control alongside self-defence skills.

Adults & Black Belts

Open classes for beginners through to a dedicated Black Belts session. Locks, throws, takedowns, chokes, striking, and kicking — taught alongside the traditional forms and self-defence philosophy behind them.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a uniform to start?

No — you can start in a tracksuit or t-shirt and joggers. A student gi costs less than £25 when you do want one.

Do I have to learn Japanese?

No. As a traditional art we use some Japanese terminology, usually with an English translation, and it becomes familiar over time.

I'm not interested in competing — is that okay?

Yes. Competing is entirely optional; we offer classes for students who want to compete, but plenty of members never do.

I just want self-defence, not the traditional side — is this still for me?

Come and try us out. Regular training builds the body and mind to use techniques when it really matters, rather than relying on a two-hour quick-fix course.

What happens in a typical class?

A brief warm-up, followed by basic techniques or combinations, work on pads and with fellow students, and practice of set forms (kata) that help commit self-defence techniques to memory.
